Costa Del Mar Sunglasses | http://www.costadelmar.com

Costa is easily the most angling centric eyewear company on the planet. As a matter of fact, they may be the ONLY company on earth that makes EXCLUSIVELY Polarized eyewear... nothing but, and all of the time.

From their origin and factory on the Florida coast to the reservoirs of the south and the trout streams of the high rockies, Costa is the definitive leader in premium angling shades.

Here at Comb we have always been a huge fan of the Costa optics, and back in the day when I was living at the Long Key campground and various guide friends' couches during the long Colorado off seasons, I used the Costa Vermillion lenses to help pick out the numerous Tarpon, Bonefish, and Permit nearby. The Costa signature red/copper tint seemed to turn all of the jade green backs of the fish brownish, and we were able to see them as never before.

Costa became my spring tradition, and those Vermillions stayed in a safe place in Telluride until each spring when Tarpon season returned. Since our initial meeting twenty plus years ago, Costa's lens technology and the width of their offerings, have grown tremendously. Their lenses now span all applications ranging from lifestyle, and skiing all the way to the bridge of a sportfisher or the bow of a dory.

Conservation and stewardship are important additional reasons that we have added Costa Del Mar to our stable of manufacturers. Costa is a proud sponsor of Trout Unlimited, Bonefish Tarpon Trust, The Billfish Foundation, and Coastal Conservation Association. This is the who's-who of organizations dedicated to protecting the widest array of our aquatic resources for the future.

I have had the good fortune to, at one time or another, use all different types and brands of eyewear in my own pursuit of fish. To now settle on Costa feels like a homecoming, and the fitting end of an exhaustive search.

Whatever other eyewear you have used, you will likely find as I did, that your search will be over when you begin using Costa. See for yourself, and see what's out there.
